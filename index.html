Background

My engineering
background.

I started in parts and assembly design at Los Alamos National Laboratory, spending five years working on weapons systems. Tight tolerances, materials, and finite element analysis.

During my final year at UNM, I picked up robotics work kinematics, linear algebra, 3D space modeling. Representing transforms in homogeneous coordinates, reasoning about how systems move through space, eventually running research for UNM Robotics Laboratory.

For the past several years I've been in power engineering. Grid operations, reliability programs, data analysis, KPI development, program management. Starting off with days in the field, gritty designs (CAD, Inventory, Redlining); eventually moving to strategic roles; multi-million program management, complex failure analysis, high-level reporting.
